Mapping the Experience (UX) on NYC Streets

Mobility can be reframed as cumulative experience, influenced by a variety of environmental, temporal and societal conditions.

We can begin to imagine User Experience as a set of interrelated experiential components, each with a specific demand or generated load on users.

Cognitive Load The mental effort required to process information, navigate environments, and make decisions. e.g. reading street signs, choosing a path at a busy intersection
Sensory Load The intensity and quality of sensory input experienced in the environment. e.g. noise levels, glare, crowding, smells
Physical Load The bodily exertion required to complete a mobility task. e.g. climbing stairs, uneven pavement, walking distance
